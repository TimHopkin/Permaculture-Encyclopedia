## üèõÔ∏è Governance Model

### Distributed Democratic Governance

#### Governance Principles
1. **Sociocracy**: Consent-based decision making for major platform decisions
2. **Subsidiarity**: Decisions made at the most local level possible
3. **Transparency**: Open governance processes with public documentation
4. **Accountability**: Regular review and feedback mechanisms for leaders
5. **Diversity**: Representation across cultures, regions, and expertise areas
6. **Sustainability**: Long-term thinking in all governance decisions

#### Organizational Structure

##### Global Governance Council (15 members)
**Composition**:
- 5 Regional Representatives (elected by regional councils)
- 3 Indigenous Knowledge Keepers (selected by indigenous communities)
- 2 Scientific Advisors (elected by research community)
- 2 Youth Representatives (under 30, elected by youth members)
- 2 Founder Representatives (original platform creators)
- 1 Donor/Funder Representative (major financial contributors)

**Responsibilities**:
- Strategic direction and major policy decisions
- Budget allocation for major initiatives
- Partnership agreements and collaborations
- Platform governance and community standards
- Annual report and transparency requirements

##### Regional Councils (5-7 members each)
**Composition**:
- Local chapter representatives
- Regional coordinators
- Cultural and linguistic diversity representatives

**Responsibilities**:
- Regional strategy and resource allocation
- Local chapter support and coordination
- Cultural adaptation and language support
- Regional partnership development

##### Local Chapters (Self-governing)
**Structure**: Flexible governance adapted to local culture and needs
**Support**: Training, resources, and coordination tools from platform
**Autonomy**: Local decision making within global community standards
